Bio

  • Early Days

    Maryruth Culver started her musical career as an accompanist and cantor for St. Stephen’s Catholic Church in Valley Center, California at the young age of 11. This was just the beginning of Maryruth’s career as a musician and vocalist performing extensively throughout San Diego County. The San Diego Union Tribune and KUSI News recognized her as one of San Diego’s up-and-coming young artists.

  • Education

    Maryruth studied music at the University of Arizona. In particular, she studied Harp under Dr. Carrol McLaughlin and graduated Magna Cum Laude in 2009 with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Harp Performance. In addition to studying harp, Maryruth also studied classical voice under Dr. Tony Antista and Meray Boustani, as well as piano under Dr. Lisa Zdechlik and jazz under Jeff Haskell.

  • Experience

    For four years Maryruth toured extensively with the world-renowned harp ensemble known as “HarpFusion” and was often featured as a composer, arranger, and vocalist with the group. Some of her international tours with “HarpFusion” include the countries of Mexico, Belgium, Holland, Lichtenstein, Austria, Switzerland and Brazil. She also gave a solo recital in 2009 in Rio de Janeiro for the IV Annual Rio Harp Festival. Her commercial work has included regular performances for the Ventana Resort in Tucson, Arizona, the Westgate Hotel in San Diego, and Nordstrom at the Westfield Mall in North County San Diego.

  • Performances

    Maryruth also performed with various recording artists in the contemporary Christian music genre including Matt Maher, Tom Booth, Jackie Francois and Tatiana Cameron. She is an accomplished orchestral performer and has been featured with various ensembles including the University of Arizona Symphony and Philharmonic Orchestras and the United States Marine Band.

  • Instruction

    Maryruth has had the opportunity to teach music to various age groups both in private and group settings. She instructed the intermediate group of jazz harp students at the 2007 Harpe Diem Music Festival (Belgium) and has been an assistant teacher for the “Arizona Harp Experience” summer camp for over four years. She has also served as Musical Director for the Patio Playhouse Youth Theatre and as Director of Student Music for the St. Thomas Moore Catholic Newman Center.

Today

Harp Lessons

What is my first lesson on harp like?

If you have never played the harp before, we will begin by getting to know the instrument. I will teach you proper posture and how to hold your hands to pluck the strings. We will also learn the names of some of the notes and get you started on how to practice and tune your instrument. You do not need to own or rent a harp before your first lesson. You are welcome to come in for a lesson to get to know the instrument, and we can discuss any questions you might have about lessons and acquiring a harp.

What type of harp do I need?

There are two main types of harps that most harpists play on (at least here in the USA). One is called the Pedal Harp (or Concert Harp), and the other is a Lever Harp (or Folk Harp). The main difference is in size and the method of playing. Pedal harps have a series of pedals on the floor that harpists use, while a Lever harp has a series of levers along the neck.
Generally, I like to start my students on a lever harp as they are lighter, smaller, and more affordable. However, this is not always the case, nor does it have to be. I will help walk you through the purchasing or renting of a harp if you need help! It is not as daunting a task as you might think at first.

Where can I get a harp?

There are many places where you can rent and/or purchase a harp. I will be happy to help you find the perfect instrument for you.

Voice Lessons

What type of training do you offer in voice?

I offer classical vocal training where students will learn techniques in breathing and proper singing that will enable them to sing classical vocal repertoire. This foundation however does not lock them into this genre. While some do perform, the vast majority of my students do not perform in classical situations. Most perform in musical theatre, jazz, or other contemporary venues. It is not my goal to make each student an opera singer (unless that is your dream), but rather to utilize good classical technique in order to enable students to best use their voice. I do not teach a particular genre or a particular “method.”
Students are required to work on a classical song of my choosing, and then are given the freedom to choose a song of their choosing to work on as well. Together, I try to teach the student that good technique is good technique no matter what you are singing.
Students are also encouraged to perform in choirs, theatrical productions, or other performances besides our studio recitals. Students are welcome and encouraged to bring music for other events to their lessons as I can often offer support.

What should I expect for my first voice lesson?

The purpose of the first voice lesson is for me to evaluate your voice (Soprano, Tenor, Mezzo, etc.) and to get an idea of who you are, and what you want to sing! So please be ready to sing your favorite song and to do some funny sounding warm ups with me! These will help me get an idea of where your voice is. From there I will give you a plan of action that is personalized to help you progress and succeed as a vocalist.

What generally happens in a voice lesson?

It depends on what we are working on, but the general structure is that we spend some time warming up. It is during these warm ups that we work on the technical stuff and is often where the magic happens! Following this we will work on repertoire, or the songs you are working on. I require all my voice students to learn to read music, so we work on this as well.

What do I need for voice lessons?

Students will generally be given a book to purchase and songs to download and bring into lessons throughout the course of study. I also encourage students to record their lessons for practice. Besides that, you just need your voice and a bottle of water!

Piano Lessons

What should I expect for my first piano lesson?

The first piano lesson is a great opportunity for me to evaluate your musical knowledge and get you started on a personalized road of study that will help you accomplish your goals as a piano player! If you are new to the piano, I will teach you about the instrument and correct posture, and we will pick out a book from the store (B&H Music) that is proper for your age and ability. The books I use generally cost between $10 and $15. From there we will learn the names of some notes and get you started on your first practice assignments!

What type of songs will I learn to play?

I personalize my lessons for each student depending on their personal goals as a musician. With that being said, there are some fundamental techniques and knowledge that every piano player should know. These things I insist on. Generally, beginning students will work through a method book in order to develop good technique and practice habits. As a student advances, I will supplement the method book with chosen pieces that will help them advance. I also encourage students to find pieces that they are excited to work on in addition to their lesson music. Basic study in music theory is also a part of every piano student's lesson.
